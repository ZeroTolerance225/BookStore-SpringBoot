About Us

Hello I am Moby Dickens and this is my story...

I used to be a normal man until one foggy May day in Ohio. I was on a business trip for my accounting firm when I came across a strange bookstore by the name of Sam Walker. The name of the bookstore was the same as its owner Sam. I was looking for a book to read in my free time, so I indulged myself with a visit. After grabbing a copy of a new book I began to chat up the owner in hopes of a discount. In the middle of our conversation he asked if I was interested in buying his bookstore. I jokingly replied that I did not have a lot of cash on hand to avoid any further offers. After my reply he mentioned that all he would charge me was one dollar since he wanted to get out of the business. I immediately said yes in hopes of being able to resell the store for a higher profit. I cannot begin to describe what a mistake that agreement was. As soon as I said yes shackles appeared on Sam.

He then continually jumped for joy as he spoke of freedom. When the shackles disappeared from his wrists, they appeared on mine. Sam then described to me the cycle of ownership the bookstore went through. Every owner needs to work at the bookstore for ten years before being able to sell the store for a dollar. Once the sale is made they will regain their freedom from the curse that had been placed on the store long ago. As the Bookstore changes ownership the name also changes to the new owners name. Then right before leaving his somberly said that he could finally see his wife and children again. Finally, he uttered a bone chilling statement that I still think of to this day. He looked at the book store and said, "She is yours now. And you will regret it every day you live in this prison."
